# Site Intelligence v3.10.0 — Live Intelligence Briefings, Narrative Context, and Publication Workflow

This release turns canonical Live Intelligence signals, reviewed alerts, and approved digests into source-linked editorial briefing drafts and publication-ready evidence packages.

## Delivered

- Signal, alert, digest, channel, and regional briefing templates.
- Deterministic source-bound draft assembly rather than autonomous interpretation.
- Claims that retain source references, source links, observation times, freshness, geography, and context routes.
- Chronology, limitations, evidence, and key-observation sections in every draft.
- Documented human review before a public briefing can be approved or published.
- JSON and Markdown publication packages.
- Provider-neutral handoffs to the Knowledge Library, Publications, Decision Studio, or download workflows.
- Public briefing directory, detail, policy, template, and export routes.
- A WordPress `[sc_live_intelligence_briefings]` surface for approved public briefings.

## Editorial and publication boundary

Site Intelligence does not invent sources, add unsupported claims, assert causation, publish drafts automatically, perform automatic WordPress publication, or write directly to WordPress. A draft is an editorial working artifact until an identified reviewer approves it with a documented reason. Handoffs prepare a bounded package for another system or a human editor; they do not perform publication.

## Preserved

- Canonical Live Intelligence source and signal contracts.
- Human-reviewed alerts, digests, watchlists, and subscriptions.
- Explicit freshness, source lineage, responsible-use notes, and last-known-good boundaries.
- Connected homepage, publication, Library, Advisory, Lab, channel, and embed surfaces.
- Aggregate-only public-value analytics and human-governed signal rotation.
- No subscriber profiles, recipient identities, advertising, or affiliate content in the intelligence workflow.
